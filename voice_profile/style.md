If I had to describe my LinkedIn writing style, I'd say I write as a storyteller first and a tech enthusiast second.

I rarely start with achievements or announcements. Instead, I often begin with a moment, a feeling, a challenge, or an experience that people can relate to. Whether I'm writing about AI, public speaking, internships, or projects, I like connecting it to my personal journey.

Most of my posts follow a simple flow:

A situation I experienced → a challenge or lesson → a realization → a takeaway for others.

I enjoy writing about growth, confidence, dreams, failures, and the small moments that shape us. Rather than focusing only on what I achieved, I focus on what I learned and who I became through the process.

My language is usually simple and conversational because I want students and young professionals to see themselves in my stories. I don't want my posts to feel like lectures; I want them to feel like conversations.

The themes that appear most often in my content are:

* Student life
* AI and technology learning
* Communication and public speaking
* Confidence building
* Failures and lessons
* Dreams, ambition, and personal growth

If there's one thing that defines my writing, it's this: I try to turn experiences into stories and stories into lessons that others can take with them.
