Before I knew what Retrieval-Augmented Generation was, I was already using it during my semester exams.

My flow was simple.
Take the PDF → Take my notes → List the important questions → Put everything into GPT → Add a structured prompt → Generate refined notes.

That process helped me achieve a 9.83 CGPA.

I never relied on the model blindly.
I always gave it context.

Around that time, I kept hearing one term repeatedly: RAG.

I didn't take it seriously at first.
Until we were asked to choose an AI elective for a college course.

I chose RAG.

LLMs hallucinate when they lack grounded context.
RAG isn't just an external database.
It's a controlled retrieval mechanism that decides what knowledge the model is allowed to use.

I had been manually simulating that for three to four semesters.

This is Day 1 of building publicly — documenting everything I learn about RAG until I earn a role in an AI firm.

#rag #ai #llms #buildinginpublic

1 simple hack to increase your LinkedIn growth.

Remember our first education started with stories —
maybe stories of the greedy man or the wise lady.

Storytelling helps in a much better way to connect with people on any platform.

When we listen to a story, we start to place ourselves in the shoes of that story.
We start to see ourselves as the lead.

If you remember after watching any movie, we all say:
I liked the hero… no I liked the heroine… no I liked the side character.

Why?

Because they see a part of themselves in that role — or part of that role is who they want to be.

This is the reason why stories connect people more easily.

So next time, add a pinch of story into your posts to have more authentic and genuine connections.

Stories have the ability to make things relatable.
